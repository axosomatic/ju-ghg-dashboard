Executive interpretation

Operational decarbonization is already strong.Scope 1 plus Scope 2 emissions fell from 15,126.52 tCO₂e in 2019 to 4,933.52 tCO₂e in 2025, driven mainly by the elimination of purchased-electricity emissions and lower stationary diesel use.
The main hotspot is now stationary diesel.Stationary diesel alone contributes 4,289.48 tCO₂e, equal to 42.54% of total 2025 emissions, making it the single highest-leverage decarbonization priority.
Value-chain emissions are material and improving.Scope 3 declined 21.61% year-on-year to 5,150.18 tCO₂e. The largest category is fuel- and energy-related activities, followed by purchased goods and services, commuting, and waste.
Target readiness is high.The 2030 operational target is already exceeded, while Scope 3 is already at 86% of its 2030 reduction target after only one full year of comparative reporting.
Reporting boundary in this dashboardMain Amman campus educational activities only. The Aqaba campus and university hospital are not yet included in the reported inventory boundary.

Method, coverage, and data notes

Important assumptions and data-governance notes that should accompany interpretation of the dashboard.

Coverage notes

Organizational boundaryThe inventory uses operational control and covers educational activities at the main Amman campus. Future years are expected to incorporate the branch campus and university hospital.
Scope 3 inclusionReported Scope 3 covers categories 1 to 7 relevant to the university and excludes downstream categories that do not apply, plus upstream transport of purchased goods where data was unavailable.
Method basisThe report states alignment with the Greenhouse Gas Protocol and says Axosomatic used a database of twenty thousand global and location-based emission factors where appropriate.

Assumption notes

Refrigerant leakageThe 2025 refrigerant leakage figure is a proxy equal to 10% of consumed refrigerant quantity, not a direct measurement of leakage.
Commuting surveysCommuting estimates are based on 2,372 survey responses and extrapolated to the university population, using 84 campus-attendance days per year.
Table reconciliationThe report contains some internal inconsistencies in certain narrative percentages and in the displayed Table 2 totals. This dashboard therefore reconciles totals using the report's source-level tables and internally consistent summary values.
